The Problem

Why eye-gaze, and why now?

Children with ASD reliably show measurable differences in where they look reduced attention to faces, more diffuse fixation patterns, and atypical visual exploration compared to typically-developing (TD) peers. These differences are an underused, objective signal that conventional specialist-dependent diagnosis does not exploit.

Challenge 01

Subjective & Slow

Conventional ASD diagnosis depends on specialist observation and questionnaire-based instruments slow, costly, and inconsistent across clinicians.

Challenge 02

The Missed Window

Therapy before age 5 leads to significantly better outcomes, yet Indian children are typically diagnosed at 3–5 years after much of that window has passed.

Our Approach

Gaze Heatmaps + Deep Learning

We classify ASD vs TD using only gaze heatmaps from a webcam-based eye tracker no specialist hardware, no clinic visit required for the screening step.
